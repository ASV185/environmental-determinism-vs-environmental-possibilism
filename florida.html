India's tropical climate is influence by its location, and the Himalayas. It is located far away from the poles. The Himalayas block cold air from Central Asia from reaching India making the climate warmer and more tropical.

In India, the summer last from March to June, the temperatures can exceed 104℉. The annual average tropical moonsoons temperatures are around 80℉, which is caused by the Himalayas, and brings rain to India.

Skin color in India is influenced by its tropical climate and geography. It is considered that skin pigmentation is an adaptive trait that has evolved according to UV radiation levels. Those that are closer to equator, but in tropical regions, have darker skin tones. People who live in colder regions, are thought to have lighter skin tones.

In India, the skin pigmentation is varies according to regions:
•Northern India: People in northernmost regions have fair skin.
•Northeastern India: People in Northeastern tend to have a yellow skin tones and facial features similar to Southeast Asians.
•Southern India: People in southern india, often have darker skin tones.
