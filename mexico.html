Mexican Insect Cuisine

Insects are a staple of Mexican Cuisine for centuries. Before the Spainards, there were Mexico's main source of protein that's high in fiber and low in fat. It is also considered cheap. Some believe insects are delicious and offer unique flavors and textures.

Some examples of insects in Mexican Cuisine: •Tacos and quesadillas: Chapulines(grasshoppers) can be eaten in tacos and quesadillas, boiled, fried, and seasoned with chile, lime, and garlic. •Guacamole: Chapulines can be eaten in guacamole. •Bar snacks: Chapulines can be eaten as bar snacks. •Spice mixes, salsas, and mole sauces: Chapulines, chicatanas, and maguey worms are ingredients in many spice mixes, salsas, and mole sauces. •Food colorant: Cochineal is used as a food colorant. •Street food stands and high-end restaurants: Chapulines are in both street foods stands and high-end restaurants. •Other foods: They can be food in variety of foods, including omelets, tamales, chocolate truffles, and sorbets. By region: •Puebla: Escamoles(Ant Larvae) eaten between March and May in Puebla. They have a delicate texture and buttery, nutty flavor. Zapotitlán: Cuchamás(Green Caterpillars): They are sold in markets, and a good crispy snack, fried with lime, chili, and onion, or stuffed into taco shells. Oaxaca: Chicatanas(Giant Winged Ants):These are a rare treat really only found in Oaxaca. You can try them pan roasted with lime, or mixed in salsas with chili,garlic,and salt. They are made in many dishes.
