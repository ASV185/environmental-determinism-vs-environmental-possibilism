Importance of Plants in Singapore

In Singapore, they incorporated plants in various buildings to fight rising temperatures. This includes adding plants to the rooftops and gardens. Plants absorb light, create shade, and release water that cools the air. They reduce the building's carbon footprint and it all saves owners on air conditioning and energy costs. Singapore pays property owners up to 50% of the cost of installing green rooftops and facades.

These buildings are some examples of the greenery buildings that are located in Singapore:
Oasia Hotel Downtown- This 27-story hotel has a natural vine-covered sunscreen on the exterior, four sky terraces, 1,793 planter boxes, and large structural cores for ventilation.
PARKROYAL on Pickering- This building was designed by WOHA, a Singapore-based architecture firm, with the idea of an "ultimate green city" that has more vegetation than if it were left to wilderness.

Asia Square- Designed by Denton Corker Marshall, this building has been called one of Singapore's greenest since opening in 2011. It uses 35% less energy than typical commercial buildings.
