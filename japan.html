Japanese Seafood and Sushi Cuisine

Japan is a small island country in East Asia, located in the Pacific Ocean. It has over 90,000 species of wildlife as of 2019. Besides other favorite dishes, one of Japanese people's traditional staples is seafood. Dishes such as sushi are considered one of the national dishes.

Sushi is not the only seafood dish in Japan. These are some of favorite seafood in Japan:
•Unagi(Grilled Eel): It is served in a rice bowl. It is fairly expensive.
•Uni(edible gonads of sea urchin): It is considered a delicacy in Japan. Usually enjoyed as raw sashimi or as a sushi topping.
•Maguro(bluefin tuna): Another delicacy in Japan. One of the most popular sushi toppings and sashimi. You can find in both sushi restaurant and fish market.
•Raw Tako(octopus):steamed octopus are in sushi topping, sashimi, and other seafood dishes. Grilled octopus is also popular.
•Fugu(Pufferfish): It is considered notoriously poisonous! For people who like to try it winter is fugu season. You need to make sure that the restaurant you eat it is certified. Pufferfish's poison can shut down a person's nervous system.
•Ikura(Salmon Roe): It is bright orange and a favorite for sushi topping and rice bowls. It is referred as red caviar, and a staple for a Japanese seafood bowl.
•Ebi(shrimp): You can find it in Japan from raw on sushi, grilled on teppan, to crispy and juicy as tempura.
